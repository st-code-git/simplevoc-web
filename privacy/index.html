3. Account & Authentication

You can sign in using Google Sign-In or Sign in with Apple. When you use a social sign-in, the respective provider shares your email address and display name with us. You can also sign in with a magic link sent to your email address.

All authentication is handled by Firebase Authentication (Google LLC). Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — necessary to perform the service contract.

4. AI Processing (Gemini API)

Your voice never leaves your device. simplevoc uses the platform's built-in or on-device speech recognition to transcribe your spoken sentence locally. Only the resulting plain text is forwarded — for example: "The weather is beautiful today" or "Ich möchte einen Kaffee bestellen". No audio file, voice recording, or audio stream is ever transmitted to our backend or to any third-party provider.

Your device converts your voice to text locally using its on-device speech recognition engine. The platform-specific service used depends on your operating system:

  • iOS / iPadOS — Apple Speech Recognition (SFSpeechRecognizer), audio processed on-device by Apple's framework
  • Android — Android SpeechRecognizer API, audio processed on-device
  • macOS — Apple Speech Recognition framework, audio processed on-device
  • Windows / Linux — WebAssembly (WASM) based speech-to-text module running entirely within the app on your computer, with no connection to any external service

Once your spoken sentence has been transcribed to text on your device, this text is sent to our backend (Firebase Cloud Functions), which forwards it to Google Gemini API for word extraction and translation.

Important: Please do not include sensitive personal data (names, addresses, health information, or identification numbers) in vocabulary you record. The app is designed for learning words and sentences, not for storing personal documents.

Google has committed contractually not to use submitted data to train its AI models. Processing takes place in Google's infrastructure, predominantly within the EU/EEA. For transfers outside the EU/EEA, Google relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission. Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR.

5. Subscriptions & In-App Purchases

Premium subscriptions are managed by RevenueCat, Inc. RevenueCat receives anonymised user identifiers, purchase history, and subscription status. It does not receive direct payment information — payment is processed entirely by Apple (App Store) or Google (Play Store).

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR for contract performance; Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR for legal retention obligations.

6. Device Permissions

simplevoc requests the following device permissions:

  • Microphone — to capture your voice for vocabulary input. Audio is processed entirely on-device by the platform's speech recognition engine. Raw audio is never transmitted to our backend or to any third party.
  • Speech Recognition (iOS / iPadOS / macOS) — Apple's on-device framework (SFSpeechRecognizer) converts your spoken words to text before any data leaves your device.
  • Speech Recognition (Android) — Android's on-device SpeechRecognizer API converts your spoken words to text locally on your phone or tablet.
  • Speech Recognition (Windows / Linux) — a WebAssembly (WASM) module embedded in the app processes audio locally on your computer without any external connection.

You can revoke microphone permission at any time in your device or system settings. Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR — your consent via the system permission dialog.

7. Firebase App Check

We use Firebase App Check to verify that requests to our backend originate from the genuine simplevoc app and not from bots or abusive scripts. On iOS, this uses Apple's DeviceCheck/App Attest framework; on Android, Google Play Integrity. These services attest device authenticity without identifying you personally. Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — our legitimate interest in securing the service.

8. Data Storage & Infrastructure

Your vocabulary data is stored in two places:

  • On your device — in a local database (Sembast) that works without an internet connection.
  • Google Firestore — in the europe-west1 region (Frankfurt, Germany), encrypted at rest and in transit.

Backend logic runs on Google Cloud Functions and Google Cloud Run, also in europe-west1. All data transmission uses TLS encryption.

9. International Data Transfers

Most processing takes place within the EU/EEA (europe-west1). However, some third-party service providers are based in the United States:

  • Google LLC (Firebase, Gemini API) — EU–US Data Privacy Framework (adequacy decision) and Standard Contractual Clauses
  • Apple Inc. (Sign in with Apple, App Attest) — Standard Contractual Clauses
  • RevenueCat, Inc. — Standard Contractual Clauses

These safeguards ensure an equivalent level of data protection to that within the EU, as required by Art. 46 GDPR.

10. Data Retention

Your data is stored for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account (Settings → Delete Account), all personal data — vocabulary entries, settings, and your Firebase Auth account — is deleted immediately and permanently. Anonymised, aggregated word-frequency data in our shared dictionary (not linked to your account) may be retained for service improvement.

Legal retention obligations may require us to retain certain billing records for up to 7 years (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR).

11. No Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making or profiling within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR. The spaced-repetition algorithm (FSRS) that schedules your reviews operates entirely on your device and on your own data — it does not profile you or produce legal effects.

4. KI-Verarbeitung (Gemini API)

Deine Stimme verlässt dein Gerät nicht. simplevoc nutzt die geräteeigene Spracherkennung des jeweiligen Betriebssystems, um deinen gesprochenen Satz lokal in Text umzuwandeln. Nur dieser Textinhalt wird weitergeleitet – zum Beispiel: „The weather is beautiful today" oder „Ich möchte einen Kaffee bestellen". Keine Audiodatei, keine Sprachaufnahme und kein Audiostream wird an unser Backend oder an Drittanbieter übermittelt.

Dein Gerät wandelt deine Stimme lokal über seine plattformeigene Spracherkennungs-Engine in Text um. Welcher plattformspezifische Dienst genutzt wird, hängt von deinem Betriebssystem ab:

  • iOS / iPadOS – Apple Speech Recognition (SFSpeechRecognizer), Audio wird auf dem Gerät durch Apples Framework verarbeitet
  • Android – Android SpeechRecognizer API, Audio wird auf dem Gerät verarbeitet
  • macOS – Apple Speech Recognition Framework, Audio wird auf dem Gerät verarbeitet
  • Windows / Linux – WebAssembly (WASM)-basiertes Spracherkennungsmodul, das vollständig innerhalb der App auf deinem Computer läuft – ohne Verbindung zu externen Diensten

Sobald dein gesprochener Satz auf dem Gerät in Text umgewandelt wurde, wird dieser Text an unser Backend (Firebase Cloud Functions) gesendet, das ihn zur Wortextraktion und Übersetzung an die Google Gemini API weiterleitet.

Wichtiger Hinweis: Bitte gib keine sensiblen personenbezogenen Daten (Namen, Adressen, Gesundheitsinformationen oder Ausweisnummern) als Vokabeln ein. Die App ist für das Lernen von Wörtern und Sätzen konzipiert – nicht zur Speicherung persönlicher Dokumente.

Google hat vertraglich zugesichert, übermittelte Daten nicht zum Training seiner KI-Modelle zu verwenden. Die Verarbeitung erfolgt in Googles Infrastruktur, überwiegend innerhalb der EU/des EWR. Für Übermittlungen außerhalb der EU/des EWR stützt sich Google auf Standardvertragsklauseln (SCC) der Europäischen Kommission. Rechtsgrundlage: Art. 6 Abs. 1 lit. b DSGVO.
